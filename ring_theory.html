Course Description

Rings and subrings. Left, right and two sided ideals in a ring. Cosets and quotient rings. Homomorphism of rings, kernels of homomorphism. The first isomorphism theorem in rings. Prime and maximal ideals. Zorn’s Lemma. The ring of integers (mod n). Polynomial and Euclidean rings. Principle ideal domains and theory of unique factorization with particular reference to the integers and polynomial rings over a field. Conditions for irreducibility of polynomials. Gaussian integers and sums of two squares. Gauss lemma and extension of the unique factorization property to polynomial rings in several variables.
